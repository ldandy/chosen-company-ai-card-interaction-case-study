# Change-Management Mode

Use this mode for workplace change, software adoption, process changes, restructures, operating model changes, AI adoption, training rollouts, or culture shifts.

## Purpose

Design cards that help people understand the change, practise new decisions, identify support needs, surface assumptions safely, and take small practical steps.

## Safe framing

Prefer:
- What support would help?
- What condition makes this harder?
- What would make the next step clearer?
- What should someone do first in this scenario?
- Which risk needs attention?
- Which tiny experiment would reduce uncertainty?

Avoid:
- Why are people resisting?
- What are you afraid of?
- Who is blocking this?
- What do you hate about the change?
- What did leadership get wrong?
- How committed are you?

## Best card types

- Scenario card.
- Support kit.
- Spot the risk.
- Rank support needs.
- Decision fork.
- Tiny experiment.
- Explain it to someone new.
- Keep, change, remove.
- Confidence scale with private or anonymous answer.
- Red flag / green flag.

## Sample change cards

Card type: Pick 2 of 5
Prompt: Pick 2 supports that would make this change easier: clear instructions, time to practise, a named person to ask, fewer surprises, or better examples.
Follow-up: Which support should happen first?

Card type: Scenario
Prompt: A team understands the goal but keeps slipping back to the old process. What is the most useful first step?
Follow-up: What would make the new process easier to remember?

Card type: Spot the risk
Prompt: Which adoption risk is most likely: unclear purpose, time pressure, poor training, old habits, or missing feedback?
Follow-up: What would reduce that risk?

Card type: Tiny experiment
Prompt: Choose one small test that could make this change easier to try for one week.
Follow-up: What would make the test safe to stop or adjust?

## Capture guidance

If capturing responses:
- State the purpose before the activity.
- Capture themes, not personal disclosures.
- Do not use responses for performance assessment.
- Do not promise anonymity unless it is real.
- Separate consultation, HR, health and safety, and formal training obligations from the card activity.
