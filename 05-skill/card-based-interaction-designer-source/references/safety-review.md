# Safety Review and Rewriting Rules

## Review questions

Ask these questions for each card:

1. Can this be answered lightly?
2. Could this embarrass, shame, expose, or isolate someone?
3. Does it ask for private information?
4. Does it invite blame, gossip, or negative ranking?
5. Does it suit the audience age and relationship level?
6. Is the wording clear in under 10 seconds?
7. Is the prompt useful enough to justify being asked?
8. Does it need a pass option, anonymous mode, or facilitation?

## Workplace checks

Add these checks for teams, training, culture, onboarding, and change-management contexts:

1. Could a manager hear this answer and judge someone?
2. Could this be mistaken for consultation, HR, or performance evidence?
3. Does it ask staff to disclose fear, frustration, or resistance publicly?
4. Does it frame people as the problem instead of conditions, systems, supports, or choices?
5. Does it create psychological safety or only pretend to?
6. Is answer capture ethical, clear, and proportionate?

## Flag labels

Use these labels:

- Keep: clear, safe, useful.
- Revise: useful idea, weak wording.
- Remove: unsafe, repetitive, unclear, or not useful.
- Facilitate only: suitable only with a skilled facilitator.
- Private only: better for journaling or confidential reflection.
- Anonymous only: useful but risky if named.

## Rewrite patterns

### Turn confession into choice
Risky: What are you worried about?
Safer: Pick the support that would make this easier.

### Turn blame into conditions
Risky: Why did the team fail to adopt the system?
Safer: Which condition makes adoption harder: time pressure, unclear purpose, poor training, old habits, or missing support?

### Turn personal vulnerability into scenario
Risky: When have you struggled with change?
Safer: A team is struggling with a change. What would help first?

### Turn vague into specific
Weak: How do you feel about AI?
Better: Pick one AI task you would trust first: summarising, drafting, checking, planning, or finding information.

### Turn moral judgement into trade-off
Weak: What is the best value?
Better: Pick 2 values that matter most when a team is under pressure.

## Red-flag topics

Avoid or handle outside cards:

- Active conflict.
- Trauma, grief, health, addiction, body image.
- Personal finances, debt, inheritance.
- Performance issues.
- Job security, restructuring details, compensation.
- Private relationship or family conflict.
- Political or religious debate unless the group explicitly wants and can safely handle it.
